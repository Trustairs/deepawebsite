About Deepa

Where finance meets sustainability

Deepa Rao is the Environment & Sustainability Governance and Reporting Principal at Cognizant, a global leader in technology and consulting services. With over 14 years of experience in internal audit, internal controls, and risk management, she has transitioned into an accomplished ESG practitioner, combining her Chartered Accountant (CA) and Master of Commerce (M.Com) credentials with deep expertise in ESG governance and reporting.

"The governance challenge is clear: Directors are approving disclosures shaped by complex systems they don't govern, with data they haven't seen, and logic they cannot fully explain."

At Cognizant, Deepa spearheads ESG governance efforts, ensuring alignment with global frameworks like GRI, SASB, and TCFD, and adherence to emerging regulations like CSRD and SEC. She also implemented a comprehensive data management system, enabling accurate measurement and reporting of ESG performance.

Part of an award-winning team recognised for leading practices in the ESG space, Deepa also serves as a board member at technology companies and as an audit committee member at educational institutions — focusing on sustainability, audit and risk, and the ethical use of AI.

Framework One

The TRUST Framework

Five core pillars providing end-to-end oversight of sustainability reporting — giving boards genuine confidence in non-financial disclosures.

T

Transparency

Data, assumptions, and methodologies must be traceable, explainable, and well-documented — reducing the risk of greenwashing.

R

Responsibility

Clear governance roles. Boards must have visibility into who owns each part of the reporting chain, from data capture to disclosure sign-off.

U

Understanding

Boards must invest in sustainability literacy — regulatory developments, data limitations, and the evolving role of technology in reporting.

S

Security & Sustainability

Information systems must be secure, resilient, and appropriately governed. Cyber integrity and data governance are board-relevant risks.

T

Trustworthiness

Reporting output must be capable of independent validation — embedding controls, enabling auditability, maintaining consistency over time.

Aligns with COSO · CSRD · GRI · SASB · ESRS

Framework Two

The AIRS Framework

Four pillars of AI governance bringing visibility and discipline to areas that are often opaque — safeguarding boards in the age of AI-powered disclosures.

A

Accountability

Clear ownership of AI systems, including vendor-supplied tools. Oversight, ethical use, and risk escalation must be explicitly assigned.

I

Integrity

AI models must be bias-tested, ethically aligned, and consistently applied. Material disclosures must meet stringent assurance standards.

R

Reporting

AI-generated insights must be explainable, traceable, and audit-ready. Every AI-influenced disclosure must be logged and attributed.

S

Stewardship

AI systems must be governed across their full lifecycle — from deployment to retirement — with ongoing board review and assurance reporting.

Aligns with ISO 42001 · NIST AI · Responsible AI Principles
